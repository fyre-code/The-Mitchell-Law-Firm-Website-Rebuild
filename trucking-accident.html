Why Trucking Cases Are Different

Crashes involving tractor-trailers, semis, and other commercial vehicles are fundamentally different from typical car accident cases. The stakes are higher, the injuries are often more severe, and the legal landscape is more complex.

Commercial trucking is governed by federal regulations issued by the Federal Motor Carrier Safety Administration (FMCSA), which impose requirements on driver qualifications, hours of service, vehicle maintenance, and cargo loading. When a trucking company or driver fails to comply — and someone gets hurt — those violations become critical evidence.

Multiple parties may share liability in a trucking accident: the driver, the trucking company, a maintenance contractor, a cargo loader, or even the truck manufacturer. Trucking companies often carry large insurance policies and deploy experienced defense teams immediately after a crash to investigate and limit their exposure.

  • Emergency and ongoing medical treatment
  • Lost income and reduced earning capacity
  • Pain, suffering, and emotional distress
  • Permanent disability or disfigurement
