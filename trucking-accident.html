Why Trucking Cases Demand Immediate Action

Commercial trucking companies and their insurers respond to serious accidents quickly — often dispatching investigation teams to the scene before the injured party has even left the hospital. They work to collect evidence, document the scene on their terms, and begin building a defense. Waiting to consult an attorney puts you at a significant disadvantage.

Joe Mitchell III represents individuals injured in trucking accidents throughout Florida. He knows how carriers and their insurers operate, and he works to level the playing field from day one.

Steps to Take After a Trucking Accident

  • Contact law enforcement and get an official accident report
  • Seek medical evaluation promptly — even if you feel okay
  • Document the scene, vehicles, and your injuries with photos and video
  • Gather witness contact information
  • Preserve all insurance correspondence and documentation
  • Do not give a statement to any insurer before consulting an attorney

Common Causes of Trucking Accidents

  • Driver fatigue and hours-of-service violations
  • Distracted or impaired driving
  • Improper cargo loading or securement
  • Vehicle maintenance failures
  • Unsafe speed or lane changes
  • Violations of federal motor carrier safety regulations

Who May Be Liable?

Under Florida law, responsibility in a trucking accident may extend well beyond the driver. Trucking companies can be held liable when they:

  • Violated federal safety regulations
  • Failed to properly hire, train, or supervise drivers
  • Neglected required vehicle maintenance
  • Allowed or incentivized unsafe driving practices

Other potentially liable parties may include cargo loaders, maintenance contractors, and vehicle manufacturers, depending on the facts of the case.
