Privacy Policy

Last Updated: December 13, 2025

Consumer Communication

The Practice of Law is a highly personal profession where your information and your privacy is of the utmost importance. In order for The Mitchell Law Firm to ensure that your inquiries are handled accurately, courteously, and promptly, any telephone communications between you and The Mitchell Law Firm or any of our affiliates, agents, case managers, paralegals, and/or attorneys may be recorded.

The Mitchell Law Firm, P.A. may place calls or send text messages using prerecorded/artificial voice messages and/or an automatic telephone dialing system or an artificial intelligence intake system or other artificial intelligence systems to communicate with you. By providing your telephone number(s), you confirm that you are permitted to receive calls at each of the telephone numbers you have provided to us.

General

The Mitchell Law Firm, P.A. and its affiliates (referred to herein as "TMLF", "we", "us", or "our") respects your privacy and is committed to complying with this privacy policy ("Privacy Policy"), which describes what information we collect about you, how we use it, with whom we may share it, and what choices you have regarding our use of your information.

This Privacy Policy applies to the website TMLF.Law and any other associated webpages, mobile applications, and other online offerings (collectively, the "Services") that link to this Privacy Policy.

Types of Information We Collect

"Personal Information" refers to information that identifies you, either alone or in combination with other information. Examples of Personal Information include but are not limited to, name, email address, phone number, and mailing address.

When representing clients, Personal Information also includes certain information we collect when you retain TMLF to represent you in legal matters, including the distribution of settlement or other payments to you, if applicable. This information can include your Social Security number and financial account numbers.

You may provide Personal Information to us — such as your name, e-mail, phone number, and zip code — when you contact us, create an account, or request a consultation. The information may be required to respond to your request for consultation or representation.

We may also collect information automatically through cookies and other technologies such as log files, tracking pixels, and analytic tools and services. This includes your IP address, location information, the type of the web browser and operating system you use, browsing activities, and usage information.

Cookies

A cookie is a small piece of data stored by your web browser on your computer or mobile device. We use cookies to remember user preferences and settings, personalize your experience with the Services, and for security purposes. You may disable cookies through your browser settings, though doing so may disable many of the portions, features, or functionality of the Services.

Online Analytics

We use third party analytics tools, such as Google Analytics, in order to better understand your use of our Services and how we can improve them. You can opt out of Google Analytics by installing the Google Analytics opt-out browser add-on.

We may use your Personal Information to:

  • Facilitate requests for case evaluation and provide legal services
  • Create and manage accounts
  • Respond to your inquiries and communicate with you, including by placing calls or sending texts using any automated technology, including prerecorded messages
  • Send newsletters and marketing communications
  • Operate, maintain, and improve the Service
  • Understand how users interact with the Service
  • Improve the effectiveness of our advertisements
  • Evaluate job applicants and hire personnel
  • Enforce our Terms of Use and other agreements

  • Protect the rights, property, and security of TMLF and others

How We May Share Your Personal Information

We may share your Personal Information with:

  • Service providers in connection with the consideration, negotiation, or completion of a transaction in which TMLF is acquired by or merged with another business
