Introduction

A slip and fall accident can lead to serious injuries, unexpected medical bills, and time away from work. These incidents often happen because a property owner failed to maintain safe conditions. Joe Mitchell III, a Florida Bar Board Certified Civil Trial Lawyer, helps injured clients understand their rights and determine whether a property owner may be responsible.

What to Do After a Slip and Fall Accident

Taking the right steps after a fall can strengthen your claim.

1

Report the incident to the property owner or manager

2

Take photos or videos of the dangerous condition

3

Get names and contact information of witnesses

4

Document your symptoms and seek medical care promptly

5

Keep records of medical bills, missed work, and expenses

6

Speak with an attorney before dealing with insurance

These steps help preserve important evidence.

Common Causes of Slip and Fall Accidents

Slip and fall injuries can occur in many settings, including stores, restaurants, parking lots, apartment complexes, offices, and private homes. Common causes include:

  • Wet or slippery floors without warning signs
  • Uneven sidewalks or flooring
  • Poor lighting
  • Loose rugs or mats
  • Cluttered walkways
  • Broken steps or handrails

Identifying the cause helps determine whether the property owner failed to provide a reasonably safe environment.

Property Owner Responsibility in Florida

Under Florida law, property owners must take reasonable steps to keep their premises safe. They may be responsible if:

  • They created a dangerous condition
  • They knew about the hazard and failed to fix it
  • The condition existed long enough that they should have known about it
  • They failed to warn visitors about risks

Joe can review the facts and explain whether the circumstances meet the legal standard for a premises liability claim.

How an Attorney Can Help With a Slip & Fall Case

Slip and fall cases often involve complex questions about liability, notice, and damages. An attorney can:

  • Investigate what caused the fall
  • Gather evidence such as photos, reports, and witness statements
  • Review medical documentation
  • Handle communication with insurance companies
  • Explain available damages and possible next steps
