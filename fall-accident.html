Slip, Trip, and Fall Injuries in Florida

Falls caused by hazardous conditions on someone else's property can result in serious, life-altering injuries — broken bones, head trauma, spinal injuries, and long-term mobility limitations. If you were hurt because a property owner failed to maintain a safe environment, you may have a claim under Florida premises liability law.

Florida law imposes a duty on property owners to maintain reasonably safe conditions for visitors and guests. When they fail to identify, correct, or warn about dangerous conditions — wet floors, uneven surfaces, poor lighting, broken stairs, inadequate security — and someone is injured as a result, they can be held accountable.

Common Types of Fall Accidents Joe Handles

  • Slip and fall on wet or slippery floors
  • Trip and fall on broken or uneven pavement
  • Falls caused by defective stairs or handrails
  • Falls in grocery stores, retail stores, or restaurants
  • Falls in parking lots or common areas
  • Falls caused by poor or missing lighting

Building a Strong Premises Liability Case

These cases are often more complex than they appear. Property owners and their insurers will investigate quickly, dispute the hazard existed, or argue you were partly at fault. Joe works to gather evidence while it is still available — incident reports, surveillance footage, maintenance records, and witness accounts — and builds a case around the specific facts that matter.
