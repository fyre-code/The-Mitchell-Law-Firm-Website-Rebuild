Understanding Florida's Wrongful Death Act

When a person dies due to the wrongful act, negligence, default, or breach of contract or warranty of another person or company, Florida's Wrongful Death Act provides surviving family members with a legal mechanism to seek compensation for their loss.

A wrongful death claim is separate from any criminal proceedings related to the death. It is a civil matter — one that focuses on the financial and personal losses suffered by the surviving family, and on holding the responsible party accountable through the civil justice system.

Who Can Bring a Wrongful Death Claim in Florida?

Under Florida law, a wrongful death action must be filed by the personal representative of the deceased's estate. Damages may be recovered on behalf of:

  • The surviving spouse
  • Minor children
  • Adult children (in certain circumstances)
  • Parents of the deceased (in certain circumstances)
  • Other blood relatives who depended on the deceased

What Damages Are Available?

Depending on the facts and the survivors involved, damages in a wrongful death case may include:

  • Loss of support and services provided by the deceased
  • Loss of companionship, instruction, and guidance
  • Mental pain and suffering of survivors
  • Medical and funeral expenses
  • Lost earnings and earning potential of the deceased
