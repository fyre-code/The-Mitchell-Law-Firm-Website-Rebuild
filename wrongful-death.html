Helping Families Seek Justice Under Florida Law

The Mitchell Law Firm, led by Joe Mitchell III, specializes in wrongful death cases in Brevard County, Florida. Joe helps families navigate claims arising from negligence or wrongful conduct that resulted in a loved one's death — reviewing circumstances, explaining Florida statutes, gathering evidence, and supporting families through what is often the most difficult period of their lives.

Common Causes of Wrongful Death

  • Vehicle accidents — cars, motorcycles, and commercial trucks
  • Workplace incidents and occupational accidents
  • Unsafe property conditions (premises liability)
  • Defective products
  • Negligence or intentional misconduct

Who May File a Wrongful Death Claim in Florida?

Under the Florida Wrongful Death Act, a claim must be brought by the personal representative of the deceased's estate, on behalf of eligible survivors — which may include:

  • A surviving spouse
  • Children of the deceased
  • Parents of the deceased (in certain circumstances)
  • Other blood relatives or adoptive siblings who were dependent on the deceased

Types of Recoverable Damages

Damages in a wrongful death case may include:

  • Medical expenses related to the fatal injury
  • Funeral and burial costs
  • Lost financial support the deceased would have provided
  • Loss of companionship, guidance, and protection
  • Mental pain and suffering of eligible survivors
