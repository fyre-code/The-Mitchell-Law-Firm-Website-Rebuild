Protecting Your Reputation Under Florida Law

False statements that damage your reputation, career, or relationships can cause lasting, measurable harm. Joe Mitchell III is a Florida Bar Board Certified Civil Trial Lawyer who handles defamation claims for both individuals and businesses — reviewing statement content, evaluating applicable legal standards, identifying defendants and defenses, and communicating with opposing parties on your behalf.

Steps to Take After Discovering a Defamatory Statement

  • Preserve screenshots and documentation of the statements immediately
  • Record the date, time, and platform or location where the statements appeared
  • Identify the parties responsible for making or publishing the statements
  • Avoid making public responses that could escalate the situation
  • Keep all related communications and correspondence
  • Document any measurable impacts on your reputation, career, or finances
  • Consult an attorney before responding publicly or contacting the other party

Common Sources of Reputational Harm

  • False statements published online or on social media
  • Damaging negative reviews that contain false factual claims
  • Allegations made to employers or professional licensing boards
  • False accusations shared with co-workers, clients, or third parties
  • Inaccurate publications in news or media outlets
  • Statements made during employment disputes

Florida Defamation Standards

A defamation claim in Florida generally requires proof that:

  • A false statement of fact (not opinion) was made about you
  • The statement was communicated to at least one third party
  • The person who made the statement acted with negligence or malice, depending on your status as a public or private figure
  • The statement caused damage to your reputation, business, or livelihood

Certain categories of defamation — including false accusations of a crime or statements that harm a person's profession — may be considered defamatory "per se," allowing claims to proceed without proof of specific financial harm.
